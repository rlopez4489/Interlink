EB-5 visa for investors

The purpose of the EB-5 visa is to encourage foreign investment and economic growth in the United States. For this reason, it is the only visa that requires a specific amount of investment.

Requirements to apply for the EB-5 visa

Unlike other visas that require the investor to have a nationality of a country that maintains a Free Trade and Navigation Agreement with the United States, the EB-5 visa is available to all foreign citizens who meet its requirements.
To qualify for the EB-5 visa, you must invest at least $800,000 USD in a designated regional center or $1.5 million USD anywhere in the United States.
Regardless of the size of the investment, the investor must create full-time jobs for at least 10 US workers.

Benefits of applying for the EB-5 visa

Unlike other visas, the EB-5 visa allows the investor, their spouse and children under the age of 21 (unmarried), to obtain permanent residence, known as the Green Card.
Your children will be able to study in the United States in both public and private schools or universities..
Lawful permanent residents with the EB-5 visa program will enjoy the same benefits as any other United States resident, except the right to vote.
Investors are not required to participate in the day-to-day management of their investment. By applying for an EB-5 visa, they can do so as limited liability partners and engage in other companies/businesses.
The investor and his family are free to return to their country for visits or business. However, they must maintain a domicile in the United States.
