E-1 visa for investors

The E-1 nonimmigrant visa classification allows a national of a trade treaty country to be admitted to the United States solely to engage in international trade.

Requirements to apply for the E-1 visa

To be eligible for the E-1 visa, the treaty trader must demonstrate that he or she is a citizen of a country that maintains a Free Trade and Navigation Agreement with the United States.
The trade is of a substantial nature and is conducted between the United States and the treaty country that qualified the trader.
If the trader wants to bring in an employee to qualify for the visa E-1, the trader must have the same nationality as the employer, special skills and/or aptitudes that make the employee's services essential to the efficient operation of the treaty business commercial.

Benefits of applying for the E-1 visa

Freedom to travel, the E-1 visa, allows you to travel freely to any part of the United States, in addition to leaving and entering as many times as necessary.
You will be able to work for your commercial company and live in that country with your spouse and unmarried children, under 21 years of age.
Spouses and children may apply for E-1 nonimmigrant visa classification as dependents and, if approved, will generally be granted the same period of stay as the employee.
You will be able to cut out intermediaries and reduce costs. For example, the goods that your company produces in the country of origin can be sold directly to your US company without intermediaries.
