Invest to obtain an investor visa

The investor visa offers an excellent opportunity for those who wish to live and undertake in the United States.

E-2 investor visa

Obtaining it is relatively easy and fast, it offers an excellent opportunity for all those foreign investors who wish to live in the United States and develop a business or company.

To apply for the E-2 visa as an investor, you must be a citizen of a country that Free Trade and Navigation Agreement with the United States.

The investment to be made must be substantial and active, there is no exact number, but it is recommended that it be between $120,000 USD and $150,000 USD, these funds must be irrevocably allocated to the business or commercial enterprise, likewise the business must generate income significantly greater than those necessary only to support the investor and his family.

EB-5 investor visa

It is the only visa that establishes a specific investment amount, it allows the applicant investor and his dependents to obtain the green card, that is, to be permanent residents of the USA, in principle it is granted with a conditional basis of two years.

The purpose of this program is to stimulate the economy of the United States to a greater extent. The applicant must meet certain terms:

Have established a new commercial company.
Risked your capital, to the date +$800,000 USD is required
Create 10 full-time jobs.
As an investor you must intend to remain in the USA.
Manage the company.

E-1 investor visa

It is known as the non-immigrant Treaty Trader visa.

It will be granted to those entrepreneurs or individuals who carry out commercial exchanges between the United States and their country of origin.

You qualify if:
You are an executive or a manager or a specialist employed by that company.
Owns at least 50% of the shares
As a citizen of a treaty country that is already in the US, you will be an immediate candidate for an E-1 visa holder.

L-1 investor visa

It is divided into two, L-1A y L-1B, they are available to intra-company transferees in managerial positions or who have specialized knowledge.

They can be awarded when an employer requires that a qualified employee be able to work and live in the United States.

To qualify you must:
Have worked for an eligible organization abroad for a period of one continuous year and within the three years prior to admission to the US.
Enter the United States with the intention of rendering services in an executive or managerial capacity in a branch of the same employer.
